## The Underground Advantage

### Why Underground?

Nuclear astrophysics requires measuring cross sections at stellar energies — the **Gamow window** — where the Coulomb barrier suppresses cross sections to femtobarns (10⁻¹⁵ barn) or smaller. At surface laboratories, cosmic ray-induced background overwhelms the tiny reaction signal.

**LNGS underground shielding**:
- Depth: **1,400 m** of rock below Monte Aquila
- Shielding equivalent: **3,800 m water equivalent (m.w.e.)**
- Cosmic ray flux reduction: **10⁶ times** compared to surface
- Muon flux at LNGS: ~1 muon/m²/hour (vs. ~10,000 at surface)

**Result**: LUNA can measure reactions requiring millions or billions of particle collisions to register a single event — impossible at the surface.

## Scientific Programs

### 1. Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN)

Reactions forming light elements (H, He, Li) in the first minutes after the Big Bang.

**Key LUNA reactions**: D(p,γ)³He, ³He(³He,2p)⁴He, ²H(α,γ)⁶Li, ⁶Li(p,γ)⁷Be

**Landmark result**: D(p,γ)³He measured in Nature (2020) — provided independent baryon density of the Universe, in excellent agreement with CMB (Planck).

### 2. Solar Hydrogen Burning — pp Chain

- ³He(α,γ)⁷Be: beryllium-7 neutrino production
- D(p,γ)³He: deuterium burning rate

### 3. CNO Cycle

The Carbon-Nitrogen-Oxygen cycle dominates energy production in stars more massive than the Sun and controls stellar ages via turnoff luminosity.

**Key LUNA reactions**: ¹⁴N(p,γ)¹⁵O *(rate-limiting step)*, ¹⁵N(p,γ)¹⁶O, ¹⁷O(p,γ)¹⁸F, ¹⁷O(p,α)¹⁴N, ¹⁸O(p,γ)¹⁹F, ¹⁸O(p,α)¹⁵N, ¹²C(p,γ)¹³N, ¹³C(p,γ)¹⁴N

**Landmark result**: New ¹⁴N(p,γ)¹⁵O rate → revised age of globular clusters (2004). The 12C/13C stellar ratio revised via combined ¹²C(p,γ)¹³N + ¹³C(p,γ)¹⁴N measurement (PRL 2023).

### 4. NeNa and MgAl Cycles

Secondary hydrogen burning cycles operating in AGB stars and novae, controlling Ne, Na, Mg, Al stellar abundances and ejecta.

**Key LUNA reactions**: ²²Ne(p,γ)²³Na, ²³Na(p,γ)²⁴Mg, ²⁰Ne(p,γ)²¹Na, ²¹Ne(p,γ)²²Na, ²⁵Mg(p,γ)²⁶Al

### 5. Helium Burning — Neutron Sources for the s-Process

The ¹³C(α,n)¹⁶O and ²²Ne(α,n)²⁵Mg reactions are the two neutron sources that drive the slow neutron capture (s-process), responsible for producing half of all nuclei heavier than iron.

**Landmark result**: ¹³C(α,n)¹⁶O direct cross section at s-process Gamow peak (PRL 2021) — first direct measurement at stellar energies.

### 6. Carbon Burning (Bellotti IBF Priority)

¹²C+¹²C fusion is the first step beyond helium burning in massive stars (and in Type Ia supernova progenitors). Its rate is poorly known at stellar energies due to the enormous Coulomb barrier.

**LUNA status**: Detector characterization (GePD2 HPGe + NaI array) completed. Full measurement campaign in preparation. Target energy: well below current limit of E_cm = 2.1 MeV.

## LUNANOVA — The New ERC Synergy Project (2026–2032)

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Grant | ERC Synergy Grant 2025 |
| Budget | €14 million total (~€6.2M to INFN) |
| Duration | 6 years, starting 2026 |
| Goal | Eliminate nuclear physics uncertainty from standard solar model; build a new solar model |
| PI (coordinating) | Daniel Bemmerer (Helmholtz-Zentrum Dresden-Rossendorf, HZDR) |
| PI | Alba Formicola (INFN Roma) |
| PI | Gianluca Imbriani (Università degli studi di Napoli Federico II) |
| PI | Aldo Serenelli (Instituto de Ciencias del Espacio, ICE-CSIC, Spain) |
| Experimental sites | LNGS Gran Sasso (Italy) + Felsenkeller (Dresden, Germany) |
| Science | Solar fusion reactions (hydrogen → helium) at Gamow energies; combine nuclear data with solar neutrino and helioseismology observations |

**Motivation**: The solar composition problem — discrepancy between solar model predictions (elemental abundances from spectroscopy vs. helioseismology) — currently has nuclear physics uncertainties as the dominant limiting factor. LUNANOVA aims to resolve this by providing precise reaction rates for all key pp-chain and CNO reactions.
