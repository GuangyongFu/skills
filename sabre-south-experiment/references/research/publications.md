## Key Results by Paper

### arXiv:2603.11981 — Muon flux at SUPL (2026, Fu G. first author)
- **Result**: f = (6.33 ± 0.04_stat ± 0.35_sys) × 10⁻⁸ s⁻¹ cm⁻²
- **Method**: 8 EJ200 panels in telescope mode, 236 days (Apr 2024 – Mar 2025)
- **Significance**: SUPL confirmed comparable shielding to Boulby (UK) and LNGS (Italy)
- **Systematic vs theory**: Experimental uncertainty 1 order of magnitude below overburden simulation uncertainty
- **PMT config**: Hamamatsu R13089, dual-PMT geometric mean for position-independent energy

### arXiv:2411.13889 — TDR Executive Summary (2025, Barberio et al.)
- Total background target: 0.72 cpd/kg/keVee (1–6 keV ROI)
- Crystal mass: 7 × 5 kg (RMD) or 7 × 7 kg (SICCAS) = 35–50 kg
- Light yield: ~12–14 pe/keV
- Sensitivity: 5σ discovery / 3σ exclusion of DAMA-like signal in 2 years
- LS veto: ~11,600 L LAB + PPO + bis-MSB, 18–32 R5912 PMTs
- Shielding: 112 t total (8 cm steel + 10 cm HDPE + 8 cm steel)

### arXiv:2205.13849 — Background simulation (EPJC 2023)
- Dominant background below 6 keV: 40K (γ) in crystals, vetoed by LS
- External gamma background after shielding: ~10⁻⁴ cpd/kg/keVee
- LS veto reduces 40K contribution by ~10×, suppresses total background by 27%
- Geant4-based simulation using DECAY0 for radioactive decays

### arXiv:2504.17209 — R11065-20 PMT characterization (JINST 2025)
- Crystal PMTs: Hamamatsu R11065-20 (76 mm, ≥30% QE)
- Measured: single-PE response, gain, dark rate, timing, afterpulsing, dynode glow
- BDT machine-learning classifier for noise discrimination in 1–2 keVee range

### arXiv:2505.10353 — LS Veto PMT pre-calibration (JINST 2025)
- Veto PMTs: Hamamatsu R5912 (204 mm)
- Pre-calibration performed before underground installation
- Characterized: gain uniformity, SPE resolution, time response
