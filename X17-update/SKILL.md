## Origin: The ATOMKI Anomaly

### The Signal (2016–2022)

The Hungarian Institute for Nuclear Research (ATOMKI, Debrecen) observed anomalous electron-positron pair production in three nuclear transitions:

| Nucleus | Transition | Year | Significance | Reference |
|---------|-----------|------|-------------|-----------|
| ⁸Be | 18.15 MeV* → 0⁺ g.s. | 2016 | ~6.8σ | PRL 116, 042501 (arXiv:1504.01527) |
| ⁴He | 21.01 MeV* → 0⁺ g.s. | 2019 | ~7.2σ | PRL 128, 042501 |
| ¹²C | 17.23 MeV* → 1⁺ | 2022 | ~4.6σ | Phys.Rev.C (2022) |

**Common signature**: Excess of e⁺e⁻ pairs at large opening angles (≳100°), incompatible with standard Internal Pair Creation (IPC). All three excesses are consistent with production + decay of an intermediate boson: **nucleus* → nucleus + X17 → nucleus + e⁺e⁻**.

**Fitted X17 mass** (nuclear data alone): ~16.7–17.0 MeV/c²
**Best combined mass** (nuclear + PADME): **16.88 ± 0.05 MeV** (arXiv:2504.11439)

### Latest ATOMKI Results (2024)

A 2024 update paper (arXiv:2409.16300, Krasznahorkay et al.) presents:
- Peak-like anomalies confirmed in ⁸Be, ⁴He, and ¹²C
- New results from Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) decay of ⁸Be
- New high-efficiency e⁺e⁻ pair spectrometer deployed (Nucl.Instrum.Meth.A 1084:171232, 2026)
- **Physics Run 2025**: New dedicated data collection exploiting the 1030 keV resonance in ⁸Be (ongoing/recently concluded)

## Key Experimental Results

### MEG II (PSI, Switzerland) — NULL RESULT

**Status**: Published July 2025

**Method**: Protons (Cockroft-Walton, up to 1.1 MeV) on ⁷Li target → ⁸Be* decay → e⁺e⁻ pairs. High-precision tracking with drift chamber in magnetic field. Directly replicates the ATOMKI ⁸Be setup.

**Result**:
- No significant excess observed
- Upper limit: **BR(⁸Be* → ⁸Be + X17) < 1.2 × 10⁻⁵** at 90% CL
- **Excludes ATOMKI ⁸Be signal at 94% CL**

**Significance**: First high-precision independent cross-check of the ⁸Be anomaly. Uses superior detector technology (designed for μ→eγ searches, repurposed). Data collected in 2023.

**Talk at Frascati workshop**: Cecilia Voena (INFN), 20 March 2026

---

### PADME (Frascati, Italy) — WEAK EXCESS

**Status**: Run III published May 2025 (arXiv:2505.24797, JHEP 11:007, 2025)

**Method**: Positron beam on diamond target (DAFNE LINAC). Beam energy varied 262–296 MeV → center-of-mass energy √s = 16.4–17.4 MeV. Searches for e⁺e⁻ resonant annihilation: e⁺e⁻ → X17 → e⁺e⁻. Blind analysis. Calorimeter: refurbished L3 BGO crystals from LEP.

**Run III Results**:
- Most significant deviation: √s ≈ **16.90 MeV**
- Local significance: **2.5σ**
- Global significance: **~2σ**
- Fitted mass (combined with nuclear data): **16.88 ± 0.05 MeV** (uncertainty reduced >2× vs. nuclear alone)
- Data consistent with background in most of the energy range; limits set in unexplored regions

**Run IV**: New data-taking campaign started 2025 with upgraded detector. Multiple energy points collected; campaign continues through end of 2025/early 2026.

**Conference talks**:
- Marco Mancini (INFN): "X17 Search at PADME: From Run III results to Run IV upgrades", 19 March 2026
- Paolo Valente (INFN): "PADME future and perspectives", 19 March 2026

## Theoretical Interpretations

### Preferred Quantum Numbers

| Property | Status |
|----------|--------|
| Mass | ~16.88 ± 0.05 MeV (combined fit) |
| Spin-parity | Axial vector (1⁺) most consistent with all 3 anomalies |
| Couplings | Protophobic (coupling to protons suppressed); couples to electrons and neutrons |
| Dark photon | Disfavored (isospin considerations) |
| Spin-2 | Disfavored by SINDRUM + MEG II constraints |

### Surviving Models (post-MEG II)

1. **Axial vector (1⁺)**: Most natural simultaneous explanation of ⁸Be, ⁴He, ¹²C anomalies. Requires protophobic coupling structure. Tension with NA64 at some points in parameter space.

2. **CP-even scalar (0⁺)**: Previously dismissed; MEG II null result for ⁸Be revived this option for the He/C anomalies independently. Paper: arXiv:2501.05507 (JHEP 04:035, 2025).

3. **Chiral couplings (new, 2026)**: UV-motivated models naturally produce both vector and axial-vector components simultaneously (analogous to SM weak interactions). Tension with atomic parity violation constraints; ¹²C data pushes preferred values toward excluded region. Fermilab paper arXiv:2602.11263.

## The Bottom Line

The X17 field is at maximum tension (April 2026):

1. **ATOMKI** continues to report signals in all three nuclei at high significance
2. **MEG II** directly contradicts the ⁸Be signal — same reaction, no excess
3. **PADME** shows a 2.5σ local hint at the right mass from a completely independent method
4. **Theory** cannot easily accommodate all observations simultaneously; multiple competing models survive but each has tensions

**Most likely near-term resolution**:
- PADME Run IV (2025–2026): higher statistics will confirm or refute the 2.5σ hint
- ATOMKI Physics Run 2025: new 1030 keV resonance data may sharpen the ⁸Be picture
- JLab dedicated experiment: strongest independent check after MEG II
- BESIII with 20 fb⁻¹: Dalitz decay constraints will cover significant parameter space

**Probability assessment (theoretical community consensus, 2026)**: The simultaneous explanation of all three ATOMKI anomalies by a single new particle is under significant pressure from MEG II. The most conservative interpretation is that the ⁸Be signal is challenged, but the He and C signals remain unexplained. New data in 2026 will be decisive.
